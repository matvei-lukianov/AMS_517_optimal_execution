Motivation
•In classical optimal execution literature, market liquidity and price impact are specified
exogenously
⇒Traders optimize against agiven impact process(e.g. square-root law)
⇒Stochastic control problemwith known/fixed dynamics
•In modern electronic markets, liquidity is supplied by strategic and adaptive agents.
⇒Liquidity providers update their quotes in response to the observed order flow
⇒Price impact is no longer a fixed process, but the outcome of market interactions
⇒The execution problem becomes part of astrategic equilibrium problem
Central question:How can optimal execution be studied in a market where liquidity and price
formation arise endogenously from strategic interaction among heterogeneous agents?

Why do we need a market simulator?
•Withendogenous liquidity, execution is astochastic game.
•A correct formulation requires precisetimingandinformation sets.
•Otherwise, agents may observe inconsistent states or react within the same period.
Key point:we need a simulator that enforcessynchronizationand preserves the intended
information flow, so the game is well posed.
TTU Mathematical Finance – 2026 16/29

--- Page 18 ---

Why ABIDES-MARL?
•ABIDES: alimit order book market simulatorwhere events (orders, messages) are processed
asynchronously.
•Event-driven: the market evolves as events arrive over time, not in fixed steps.
•MARL: agents act at fixed-step decision times and should observe acommon state.
This creates amismatch:
•without synchronization, agents may observedifferent states,
•one agent may react within the same period to another’s action,
•the interaction no longer matches the intendedstochastic game.
Key idea:introducesynchronized decision periodsinside the event-driven simulator.

Degenerate case: no informed trader
•If the informed trader is removed, the liquidity trader becomes apredictable forced buyer.
•Market makers anticipate this and move quotes against her.
•The resulting dynamics becomedegenerate: execution prices reflect strategic extraction rather
than meaningful liquidity provision.
Interpretation:under endogenous liquidity,informational asymmetrydirectly shapes the
effective impact process.

Takeaways
•Simulator design:ABIDES-MARL imposes asynchronized multi-agentsetting.
•Why synchronization matters:it enforces a clean filtration, avoids information leakage, and
makes the learning problem awell-posed stochastic game.
•Validation:in the extended Kyle benchmark, the framework reproducesgradual price
discoveryunder an appropriate policy constraint.
•Execution insight:under endogenous liquidity, strategies derived fromfixed-impact models
can become structurally misleading.
•Bottom line:meaningful execution analysis requires anequilibrium-consistentmarket
environment.
